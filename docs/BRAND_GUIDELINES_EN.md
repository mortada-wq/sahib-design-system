# Sahib Brand Guidelines

## Brand Story

Sahib is an Arabic-first RTL design system built to help teams deliver consistent, trustworthy, and elegant digital experiences. The system is rooted in the belief that Arabic interfaces should be designed natively for Arabic readers rather than adapted as an afterthought from left-to-right products.

Sahib combines clarity, confidence, and warmth through a visual language centered on strong structure, accessible interaction, and a distinctly modern Arabic expression.

Its core palette reflects the brand promise:

- **Dark Blue `#012c55`** for trust, stability, and depth.
- **Cyan `#04aae6`** for clarity, openness, and digital energy.
- **Orange `#FF9500`** for emphasis, motion, and action.

## Logo Usage

### Core Principles

- Always preserve strong contrast between the logo and its background.
- Use the primary dark logo on light surfaces whenever possible.
- Use a white logo variant on dark surfaces or brand gradients.
- Respect RTL composition rules when placing the logo in Arabic layouts.

### Safe Area

Maintain a clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of one key letterform in the wordmark on all sides. No text, icons, or other elements should enter this protected area.

### Minimum Size

- Digital interfaces: minimum width of 96px.
- Extremely small contexts: use a symbol-only mark only when the full wordmark is not legible.

### Incorrect Usage

- Do not recolor the logo outside the approved palette.
- Do not stretch, skew, or compress the logo.
- Do not add unapproved shadows or glow effects.
- Do not place the logo on visually noisy images without a contrast layer.

## Color Palette

### Primary Colors

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Dark | `#012c55` | Headings, primary actions, brand anchors |
| Primary Light | `#04aae6` | Focus, links, highlights, interactive accents |
| Secondary Accent | `#FF9500` | Emphasis, notifications, secondary CTAs |

### Supporting Neutrals

- `#FAFBFC` page background
- `#F5F7FA` light surfaces
- `#EAEEF2` subtle borders
- `#1A1F2E` primary text

### Semantic Colors

- Success: `#10B981`
- Warning: `#F59E0B`
- Error: `#EF4444`
- Info: `#3B82F6`

### Cloudflare Tertiary Palette

Cloudflare colors may be used as a tertiary palette for data visualization, illustrations, or supporting accents. They should never overpower Sahib’s core dark blue and cyan identity.

### Suggested Usage Ratio

- 60% neutrals and backgrounds
- 25% dark blue
- 10% cyan
- 5% orange and semantic accents

## Typography

### Primary Typeface

Sahib uses **Muna** as the primary brand typeface with two weights:

- **400 Regular** for body content and long-form reading
- **700 Bold** for headings, labels, and action-oriented UI

### Typography Principles

- Default to right-aligned content in Arabic experiences.
- Maintain generous line-height for readability.
- Keep a clear hierarchy from H1 through H6.
- Avoid overly small sizes for critical content.

### Recommended Sizes

- H1: 32px
- H2: 24px
- H3: 20px
- Body: 16px
- Caption: 12px

## Tone of Voice

Sahib’s tone should feel:

- **Clear** — direct and easy to understand.
- **Reassuring** — confident without sounding rigid.
- **Respectful** — polite and human in every state.
- **Actionable** — focused on helping users complete tasks.

### Examples

- Instead of: “Request failed”
- Use: “We couldn’t complete your request right now. Please try again shortly.”

- Instead of: “Invalid input”
- Use: “Please enter a valid email address.”

## Do’s and Don’ts

### Do

- Design Arabic interfaces natively in RTL.
- Maintain accessible contrast across text and controls.
- Use orange sparingly for emphasis.
- Keep Muna as the primary typeface in official products.
- Preserve consistent hierarchy across headings and body text.

### Don’t

- Mix RTL and LTR alignment patterns without a clear functional reason.
- Replace Sahib’s primary brand identity with tertiary accent colors.
- Lower contrast in ways that harm usability.
- Introduce multiple competing primary typefaces.
- Overload users with technical or overly long interface copy.
